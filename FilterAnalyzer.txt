======================================================
         COHN FILTER ANALYZER - HELP FILE
======================================================

OVERVIEW:
The Cohn Filter Analyzer is a professional-grade RF simulation tool 
designed specifically for evaluating and optimizing 4-pole crystal 
ladder filters for Single Sideband (SSB) transceivers. 

Unlike basic calculators that assume all crystals are identical, this 
tool processes the individual motional parameters (Butterworth-Van Dyke 
models) of 4 real-world crystals using rigorous ABCD matrix cascading. 
This allows you to accurately predict insertion loss, passband ripple, 
carrier suppression, and audio fidelity before soldering any components.

------------------------------------------------------
INPUT FIELD DESCRIPTIONS
------------------------------------------------------

CIRCUIT PARAMETERS
- Capacitors (C1 - C5): The coupling capacitors in picofarads (pF). 
  In a standard Cohn topology, all 5 capacitors are typically identical.
  In a Dishal design, the end caps (C1, C2) will differ from the inner caps.
- Motional Cap (Cm): The motional capacitance of the crystal in femtofarads (fF).
- Motional Ind (Lm): The motional inductance of the crystal in millihenries (mH).
- Motional Res (Rm): The Equivalent Series Resistance (ESR) in Ohms.
- Case Cap (Cc): The physical shunt capacitance of the crystal holder in pF.

OPTIMIZATION CONSTRAINTS
These fields dictate what the algorithm considers an "acceptable" filter 
when searching for the optimal Termination Impedance.
- Max IL (dB): The maximum acceptable signal loss through the filter. 
- Max Ripple (dB): The maximum acceptable peak-to-peak variance in the passband.
- Skirt Target (dB): The depth on the skirt used to calculate the Shape Factor. 
  (e.g., entering 40 means the algorithm optimizes for the steepest drop to -40dB).

DISHAL/HAYWARD SYNTHESIS
- Target Freq (MHz): The desired center frequency of your filter.
- Target BW (Hz): Your desired -3dB bandwidth (typically 2400 to 2700 for SSB).

ANALYSIS SETUP
- Sweep Start/End: The frequency bounds for the plotting engine (in MHz).
- Z Term Real: The resistive termination impedance applied to the filter's input/output.
- BFO Offset (Hz): The distance your Beat Frequency Oscillator is placed away 
  from the -3dB edge of the filter. Used to calculate Carrier Suppression and Audio Fidelity.
